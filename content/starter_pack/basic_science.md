# Basic Science Concepts

## The Scientific Method
1. **Ask a Question**: What do you want to find out?
2. **Do Research**: Learn what is already known.
3. **Make a Hypothesis**: Predict what you think will happen.
4. **Experiment**: Test your hypothesis.
5. **Analyze Results**: Look at what happened.
6. **Conclusion**: Was your hypothesis correct?

## States of Matter
- **Solid**: Has a fixed shape and volume. Particles are tightly packed.
  - Examples: ice, rock, wood
- **Liquid**: Has a fixed volume but takes the shape of its container.
  - Examples: water, milk, juice
- **Gas**: Has no fixed shape or volume. Particles move freely.
  - Examples: air, steam, helium

### Changing States
- Solid → Liquid: **Melting** (ice melts at 0°C / 32°F)
- Liquid → Gas: **Evaporation** or **Boiling** (water boils at 100°C / 212°F)
- Gas → Liquid: **Condensation**
- Liquid → Solid: **Freezing**

## The Water Cycle
1. **Evaporation**: Sun heats water in oceans, lakes, and rivers. Water turns to vapor.
2. **Condensation**: Water vapor rises, cools, and forms clouds.
3. **Precipitation**: Water falls back to Earth as rain, snow, sleet, or hail.
4. **Collection**: Water collects in oceans, lakes, rivers, and underground.

## The Solar System
Our solar system has 8 planets orbiting the Sun:
1. Mercury — closest to the Sun, very hot and very small
2. Venus — hottest planet, thick atmosphere
3. Earth — our home, has water and life
4. Mars — the "Red Planet," has ice caps
5. Jupiter — largest planet, has a Great Red Spot (a giant storm)
6. Saturn — famous for its beautiful rings
7. Uranus — tilted on its side
8. Neptune — farthest planet, very cold and windy

## Simple Machines
Simple machines make work easier:
- **Lever**: A bar that pivots on a fulcrum (seesaw)
- **Wheel and Axle**: A wheel attached to a rod (bicycle)
- **Pulley**: A wheel with a rope (flagpole)
- **Inclined Plane**: A ramp (wheelchair ramp)
- **Wedge**: Two inclined planes together (axe)
- **Screw**: An inclined plane wrapped around a cylinder (jar lid)
