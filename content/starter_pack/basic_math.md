# Basic Math Concepts

## Addition and Subtraction

Addition means combining two or more numbers together.
Example: 3 + 5 = 8

Subtraction means taking one number away from another.
Example: 10 - 4 = 6

### Key Properties
- **Commutative Property**: The order doesn't matter for addition.
  - 3 + 5 = 5 + 3 = 8
- **Identity Property**: Adding zero doesn't change a number.
  - 7 + 0 = 7

## Multiplication and Division

Multiplication is repeated addition.
Example: 4 × 3 = 4 + 4 + 4 = 12

Division is splitting a number into equal groups.
Example: 12 ÷ 3 = 4

### Multiplication Table (1-5)
- 1 × 1 = 1, 1 × 2 = 2, 1 × 3 = 3, 1 × 4 = 4, 1 × 5 = 5
- 2 × 1 = 2, 2 × 2 = 4, 2 × 3 = 6, 2 × 4 = 8, 2 × 5 = 10
- 3 × 1 = 3, 3 × 2 = 6, 3 × 3 = 9, 3 × 4 = 12, 3 × 5 = 15
- 4 × 1 = 4, 4 × 2 = 8, 4 × 3 = 12, 4 × 4 = 16, 4 × 5 = 20
- 5 × 1 = 5, 5 × 2 = 10, 5 × 3 = 15, 5 × 4 = 20, 5 × 5 = 25

## Fractions

A fraction represents a part of a whole.
Example: 1/2 means one out of two equal parts.

### Types of Fractions
- **Proper fraction**: Numerator is less than denominator (1/3, 2/5)
- **Improper fraction**: Numerator is greater than denominator (5/3, 7/4)
- **Mixed number**: A whole number and a fraction (1 1/2, 2 3/4)

### Adding Fractions (Same Denominator)
1/4 + 2/4 = 3/4

### Adding Fractions (Different Denominators)
1/3 + 1/4 = 4/12 + 3/12 = 7/12

## Order of Operations (PEMDAS)
1. **P**arentheses
2. **E**xponents
3. **M**ultiplication and **D**ivision (left to right)
4. **A**ddition and **S**ubtraction (left to right)

Example: 2 + 3 × 4 = 2 + 12 = 14 (multiply first!)
