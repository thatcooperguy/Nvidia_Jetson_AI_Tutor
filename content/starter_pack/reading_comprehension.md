# Reading Comprehension Skills

## What is Reading Comprehension?
Reading comprehension means understanding what you read. It is not just
reading the words — it is thinking about what they mean.

## Key Skills

### 1. Main Idea
The main idea is what the text is mostly about.
- Ask yourself: "If I could only say one thing about this text, what would it be?"
- The main idea is often in the first or last sentence of a paragraph.

### 2. Supporting Details
Supporting details are facts, examples, and reasons that explain the main idea.
- Ask yourself: "What evidence does the author give?"

### 3. Making Inferences
An inference is a smart guess based on clues in the text.
- The text says: "Maria grabbed her umbrella before leaving."
- You can infer: It might be raining or about to rain.

### 4. Cause and Effect
- **Cause**: Why something happened
- **Effect**: What happened as a result
- Signal words: because, since, so, therefore, as a result

### 5. Sequence
The order in which events happen.
- Signal words: first, next, then, after, finally

### 6. Compare and Contrast
Finding how things are alike and different.
- Compare (alike): both, also, similarly, in the same way
- Contrast (different): but, however, unlike, on the other hand

## Vocabulary Strategies
When you find a word you don't know:
1. **Context Clues**: Read the sentences around the word.
2. **Word Parts**: Look for prefixes, suffixes, or root words.
3. **Substitute**: Try replacing the word with a word you know.

### Common Prefixes
- un- = not (unhappy = not happy)
- re- = again (rewrite = write again)
- pre- = before (preview = view before)
- dis- = not/opposite (disagree = not agree)

### Common Suffixes
- -ful = full of (hopeful = full of hope)
- -less = without (careless = without care)
- -tion/-sion = act of (creation = act of creating)
- -ly = in a way (quickly = in a quick way)

## Story Elements
- **Characters**: Who is in the story?
- **Setting**: Where and when does the story take place?
- **Plot**: What happens in the story?
- **Conflict**: What problem do the characters face?
- **Resolution**: How is the problem solved?
- **Theme**: What lesson or message does the story teach?
